About Us

STEMR Inc. is the advanced research, engineering, and innovation division of STEM Resources LLC—a minority-owned, mission-driven organization focused on solving complex challenges across science, technology, national security, and public health.

Over the past decade, STEM Resources has led and supported a portfolio of federally funded projects across NASA, NSF, DOE, DTRA, DHA, CDC, USDA, and other agencies. Our work spans artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, multimodal sensing, STEM education, public health analytics, research security, and advanced data systems.

STEMR drives these research efforts forward by integrating scientific discovery with real-world engineering to build solutions that are secure, scalable, and human-centered. Our mission is simple: Empowering Lives Through Discovery.

NASA-Funded Projects

• MyFIDES: Mycelial Federated Intrusion Detection System for aviation cybersecurity (SBIR Phase I).
• NASA I-Corps Cohort: EFB cybersecurity customer discovery.
• Applied research integrating AI, federated learning, and edge security for flight systems.

NSF-Funded Work

• SPARC: National research-security planning initiative.
• AMC-TEC: Building cyberinfrastructure capacity at minority-serving institutions.
• Cloud Ecosystem in a Box: Automated data ingest + research collaboration platform.
• POSE/EACS integration: Open-source ecosystems and alternative paths into computer science.

DoD & National Security

• DTRA SBIR: GIRL Framework (Graph-based Influence Ranking & Learning).
• Army SBIR: MirrorPlan Fusion Architecture (MPFA) for multi-team operations.
• DHA/DoD: Event-based Logging and Analytics and other open-topic AI research.
• AI-enabled intent modeling and multimodal fusion for defense missions.

Public Health & Data Science

• CDC NCIPC: Polysubstance Analytics as a Service (PAaaS).
• Novel approaches to monitoring online health behavior and derived psychoactive product trends.
• AI-driven modeling to support intervention planning and policy insights.

DOE, USDA & Energy Systems

• DOE/ASCR: FAIR-FedSci and resilience modeling for scientific computing.
• USDA SBIR Concepts: Food provenance tracking, nutrition analytics, and supply-chain sensing.
• HERALD: Hierarchical Federated Learning for solar & distributed energy security.

STEM Education & Behavior Modeling

• ASPIRE: AI-driven toolkit for mathematics education research in underserved communities.
• AIDE: Intelligent educator-support tools.
• M4 and ADiCo: Context-aware behavioral modeling for high-risk populations.
• Workforce programs advancing STEM access and equity.
